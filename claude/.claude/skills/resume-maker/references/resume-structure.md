# Resume structure and ordering

## default section order for experienced engineers

1. professional summary
2. skills
3. projects
4. relevant work experience
5. optional leadership or volunteer work
6. education

## professional summary

Use it when the candidate has enough experience for a real seniority signal.

Good summaries include:

- seniority
- domain expertise
- scale
- target role direction when useful

## skills section

Rules:

- hard skills only
- group by category
- order by relevance to the target role

## experience section

Rules:

- accomplishments, not responsibilities
- recent roles get more bullets
- every bullet should have a metric or strong before-and-after framing

## projects section

Include it when a project shows skill beyond the day job or proves fit for the target role.

## education

Move it down unless the candidate is early-career or changing direction and education is central to the story.

## length

- 1 page for early-career roles
- 2 pages for most experienced engineers
- do not pad to fill space

## ATS rules

- single-column layout
- standard section headers
- text must be selectable
- no tables for core experience
- mirror important JD keywords verbatim where accurate
